So you like to learn how to draw? well there are many ways to start learning how.You can take pen and paper and start learning, watch videos, or if you're really interested you can take course online or apply for art training courses
The trick is to find the best method that will work for you.

Learning to Draw with Pen & Paper

Drawing is a skill that can be developed with practice and patience. Whether you're a beginner or looking to refine your techniques, here are some dos and don'ts to help you on your journey with pen and paper drawing.
All these rules or let call them suggestions, are from my own personal experience and might not be true to your own experience. But they sure do apply or al least will emerge at some point in you drawing journey. So when they do always remember these Do's : Don'ts
This section all drawing media, whether it pen & paper, digital drawing, painting, sculptures etc, these are sure to work.

DO :
Start with the basics : Begin with simple shapes and lines to build your confidence and understanding of form.
Practice regularly : Consistency is key. Even a few minutes each day can make a big difference over time.
Use references: study from life or photographs to improve your observation skills and accuracy.
Experiment with styles : Try out different drawing styles to find what resonates with you and to develop versatility.
Learn from mistakes : Embrace errors as part of the learning process. Analyze them to understand what went wrong and how to improve.
DON'T :
Be Impatient: Don't try to draw everything at once.
Rush the process : Good drawing takes time. Don't try to complete your artwork too quickly.
Be afraid of criticism : Constructive feedback is invaluable. Seek out opinions and learn from them.
Limit yourself to one technique : Explore various pen techniques like hatching, cross-hatching, stippling, and more.
Neglect the fundamentals : Understanding perspective, composition, and anatomy is crucial, even when drawing stylized work.
Ignore the rules : Don't be afraid to break the rules. At least be careful when doing so.
Compare your journey : Everyone learns at their own pace. Focus on your progress rather than comparing yourself to others.

The key to learning drawing is to enjoy the process. Let your creativity flow, and don't be afraid to step outside your comfort zone. Happy drawing!

Mastering Drawing Through Visual Media

Visual media offers a rich and engaging way to learn drawing. It caters to those who learn best by seeing techniques in action. Here are some tips for those embarking on their artistic journey through visual media

DO:
Watch tutorials: There are countless video tutorials available online that can guide you step-by-step through various drawing techniques.
Analyze artwork: Study the work of artists you admire. Pause videos to understand how they approach composition, shading, and detail.
Create a visual library: Collect images, videos, and artworks that inspire you and refer to them often to inform your own work.
Participate in online workshops: Many artists host live sessions where you can watch them draw in real-time and ask questions.
Use apps : There are several drawing apps that provide interactive lessons and allow you to draw directly on your device.
DON'T:
Get distracted: With so much content available, it’s easy to jump from one tutorial to another. Focus on completing one lesson at a time.
Neglect practice: Watching is helpful, but improvement comes from applying what you've learned to your own drawings.
Be passive: Engage with the content by drawing along, taking notes, or even teaching someone else what you've learned.
Forget to have fun: The joy of drawing comes from the process as much as the final product. Enjoy the visual journey!

Thriving in Art Courses

Art courses provide a structured environment for learning and honing your craft. Whether you're in a classroom or taking an online course, here's how to make the most of this experience:

DO :
Engage fully: Participate actively in classes, discussions, and critiques. The more you put in, the more you'll gain.
Practice diligently: Use the assignments to push your boundaries and practice new techniques.
Seek feedback: Regularly ask for constructive criticism from instructors and peers to improve your work.
Study the masters: Take the opportunity to study art history and the works of great artists as part of your curriculum.
Network: Connect with fellow students, instructors, and visiting artists to build relationships and learn from their experiences.
DON'T :
Be intimidated: Everyone is there to learn. Don’t be afraid to show your work and ask questions.
Procrastinate: Stay on top of your assignments and projects to avoid last-minute stress.
Ignore the basics: Even if you’re eager to create complex works, a strong foundation in the basics is crucial.
Limit your learning: Explore additional resources outside of the coursework to supplement your education.
Forget self-care: While dedication is important, so is taking care of your physical and mental health.

All my learning are from practice, therefore i have not clue how useful art courses are but if you like to try, go ahead. Art courses are a fantastic way to develop your skills in a supportive and educational setting. Embrace the journey, and let the structured learning guide you to new artistic discoveries. FAQ

This is not expert advice so take it with a grain of salt, this is from my own personal experience.
If you want personal advice head to the contact page and connect.

  • Where do I start with drawing? Start by exploring subjects that interest you and practice by copying images from those topics. As you progress, you'll naturally feel ready to explore more complex subjects.
  • How can I improve my drawing skills? Practice is key. Practice does'nt always makes perfect, practising wrong equals wrong results. A helpful tip is to use the measure and mark method, where you measure the object you're copying with a pencil or ruler and make light marks on your paper as a size guide. This always work
  • Can you explain light and shade in drawing? Understanding light and shade is crucial for adding depth to your drawings. There are resources available that can teach you about this important aspect of drawing.
  • How do I keep my drawings clean? To prevent smudging, place a spare sheet of paper under your hand while drawing. This also helps to avoid transferring oils from your skin onto the paper.
  • Who are some of the Masters of drawing? Historical masters include Leonardo da Vinci, Albrecht Durer, Michelangelo, and Raphael, among others. Studying their works can provide valuable insights into techniques and styles.
  • What materials do I need to start drawing? The essentials include a set of pencils[Fiber Castell], paper[Clairefontaine Mixed Media Pad, Stonehenge Fine Art Paper, Strathmore Bristol Drawing pad] ,and an eraser. Experiment with different pencils to understand their lightness or darkness.
  • What are some tips for digital drawing? For digital artists, using shortcuts, working with layers, and utilizing perspective grids can be very helpful.
  • How do I learn to draw from scratch? Begin by drawing basic lines and shapes. Then, transform these shapes into simple 3D objects and practice drawing them in perspective.
